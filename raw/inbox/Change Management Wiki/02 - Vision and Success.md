# Vision and Success

## Purpose

Use this page to define what success looks like after the change is live and how to explain the value to different audiences.

## Best practices

- Describe the end state from the user perspective, not only from leadership perspective.
- Translate the vision into benefits for individuals, teams, and the organization.
- Identify the mindset shifts needed for adoption, such as moving from local workarounds to shared standards.
- Choose a small set of meaningful success measures rather than a long metric list.
- Define the consequences of failure so leaders understand why sustained support matters.

## What a strong vision includes

- what will be better after the change
- what work will become easier, faster, safer, or more visible
- which groups are impacted differently
- what behaviors or ways of thinking need to change

## Useful success measures

### Adoption and usage

- percentage of target users active in the new process or tool
- usage of critical features or workflows
- reduction in use of legacy process or system

### Operational outcomes

- cycle time reduction
- fewer handoff errors or rework loops
- improved compliance or audit traceability
- faster access to information

### Experience signals

- training confidence scores
- support ticket themes and volume
- manager feedback
- survey feedback on ease of use and value

## Mindset shifts to design for

- from "my team does it its own way" to "we use a shared operating model"
- from "I only need to know my task" to "I understand the end-to-end workflow"
- from "the system is extra admin" to "the system gives visibility and control"

## Common mistakes

- defining success only as technical go-live
- using vanity metrics that do not reflect business value
- ignoring the emotional side of change fatigue or perceived loss of control
- assuming all user groups need the same message and incentives

## Review questions

- Can each audience answer "what is in it for me?"
- Do the metrics prove value, not just activity?
- Have we defined the behavior change required for success?
- Have we documented what failure would look like?
