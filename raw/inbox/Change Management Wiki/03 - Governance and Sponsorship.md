# Governance and Sponsorship

## Purpose

Use this page to define who is responsible for delivery, who sponsors the change, and who provides business and technical credibility during rollout.

## Best practices

- Separate delivery ownership from executive sponsorship, but make them visibly aligned.
- Enroll business and technical SMEs early so design decisions reflect real workflows.
- Use champions or power users to test messages, surface resistance, and model desired behavior.
- Make decision rights explicit so issues do not stall in launch week.
- Build a simple operating cadence for weekly review, risk handling, and escalation.

## Core roles

### Change lead

- owns the change management plan
- coordinates communications, training, readiness, and feedback loops

### Executive sponsor

- creates legitimacy and urgency
- removes blockers and reinforces expected behaviors
- keeps department leaders aligned

### Business owner

- validates process design against business outcomes
- prioritizes scope and approves tradeoffs

### Technical owner

- ensures systems, integrations, data, and support readiness

### SMEs and champions

- validate practical use cases
- join UAT and pilot activity
- amplify messaging within teams

### People managers

- localize messages for their teams
- reinforce timing, expectations, and behavior change

## Sponsor behaviors that matter

- repeatedly explain why the change matters
- show up in milestone communications
- ask leaders for adoption progress, not just technical status
- intervene when teams delay participation or revert to old ways

## Governance minimum viable model

- weekly rollout meeting with owners and blockers
- clear RAID log: risks, assumptions, issues, dependencies
- named decision-maker for scope and launch readiness
- escalation path for business and technical issues

## Common mistakes

- naming a sponsor who is not active or visible
- relying only on project team members and skipping business voices
- using champions too late, after key decisions are already fixed
- leaving managers uninformed and expecting adoption anyway

## Review questions

- Is there a visible sponsor with enough authority to unblock teams?
- Are business and technical SMEs both represented?
- Do champions exist in the departments most affected?
- Does everyone know who can make the final go/no-go call?
