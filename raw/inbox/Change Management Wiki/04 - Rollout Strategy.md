# Rollout Strategy

## Purpose

Use this page to choose how the change will be introduced, who goes first, and which use cases matter most in the initial rollout.

## Best practices

- Match rollout style to urgency, complexity, user readiness, and business risk.
- Prioritize use cases before features. Users adopt workflows that solve real problems.
- Sequence teams based on impact, readiness, and support capacity.
- Reuse lessons from previous rollouts in the organization.
- Keep the first release focused on the minimum experience needed for success.

## Choosing a rollout style

### Big bang

Best when:

- the old and new ways cannot run together for long
- compliance or strategic timing forces a hard cutover
- the organization can absorb concentrated support demand

Risks:

- high support load
- greater business disruption if readiness is weak

### Phased

Best when:

- teams differ in readiness or workflow complexity
- you need to validate approach and refine assets as you go
- the change can tolerate a temporary mixed-state environment

Risks:

- longer transition period
- potential confusion if old and new ways coexist too long

## How to set scope

- identify 2 to 3 critical use cases per priority team
- define what must work on day one versus later phases
- record what is intentionally out of scope
- plan for dependencies such as integrations, permissions, or policy updates

## Good prioritization criteria

- business criticality
- pain level in current workflow
- readiness of leaders and champions
- technical complexity
- support team capacity

## Common mistakes

- launching every feature to every audience at once without clear need
- picking pilot groups based only on convenience
- copying past rollout approaches without checking whether the context is different
- keeping the rollout sequence implicit instead of documented

## Review questions

- Why is this rollout style right for this change?
- Which teams should go first, and why?
- What are the top use cases that prove value early?
- What is not included in the initial scope?
