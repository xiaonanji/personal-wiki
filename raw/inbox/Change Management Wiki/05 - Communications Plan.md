# Communications Plan

## Purpose

Use this page to plan how the change will be explained, repeated, tailored, and reinforced before and after launch.

## Best practices

- Communicate early, then repeat often through multiple channels.
- Tailor messages by audience instead of sending one generic announcement.
- Use executive sponsors for urgency and department leads for local reinforcement.
- Address likely resistance directly instead of pretending it does not exist.
- Keep post-launch communication active so adoption keeps building after go-live.

## Message structure

Every message should answer:

- what is changing
- why it is changing now
- what this means for this audience
- what action is expected
- where to get help

## Audience design

Typical audience groups:

- executives and sponsors
- people managers
- highly impacted teams
- lightly impacted teams
- admins, champions, and service desk staff

Each audience may need a different level of detail, timing, and call to action.

## Recommended channels

- email for durable reference
- chat channels for reminders and Q&A
- manager cascades for team-level reinforcement
- 1:1 outreach for highly impacted individuals or resistance hotspots
- launch hub or FAQ page for one source of truth

## Communication timing

- announce early enough for people to prepare
- increase cadence near training and launch milestones
- repeat core messages in different forms
- keep sending updates after launch about wins, fixes, and next steps

## Handling resistance

Common resistance themes:

- change fatigue
- fear of losing control or expertise
- uncertainty about expectations
- concern that the new process creates more work

Best response:

- acknowledge the concern directly
- connect the change to practical benefits
- show the support available
- make leaders reinforce the new expectation consistently

## Common mistakes

- one launch email with no follow-up
- overexplaining features and underexplaining benefits
- failing to equip managers with talking points
- communicating too late for users to prepare

## Review questions

- Does each audience know what to expect and when?
- Are sponsors and managers both part of the communication plan?
- Have we planned messages for resistance themes?
- Do users know where the source of truth lives?
