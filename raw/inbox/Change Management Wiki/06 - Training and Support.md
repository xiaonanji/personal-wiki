# Training and Support

## Purpose

Use this page to design role-based training, support pathways, and confidence-building activities that help people adopt the change successfully.

## Best practices

- Design training for different roles, not one generic session for everyone.
- Define outcomes in three dimensions: what users should know, do, and feel.
- Mix learning formats so people can attend live, self-serve, and ask questions.
- Stand up visible support channels before launch.
- Keep training close enough to launch that people can apply it quickly.

## Segment training by audience

Examples:

- admins versus end users
- business users versus technical users
- managers versus individual contributors
- high-frequency users versus occasional users

## Good learning outcomes

### Know

- why the change is happening
- what has changed
- where to find guidance and help

### Do

- complete the key workflows needed on day one
- follow the new process correctly
- troubleshoot simple issues or know how to escalate them

### Feel

- confident using the new approach
- supported during transition
- clear on what success looks like

## Training formats to combine

- instructor-led sessions
- train-the-trainer sessions
- office hours or drop-in Q&A
- self-paced online learning
- short task-based videos or guides

## Support design

- create a visible support channel such as Teams, Slack, or helpdesk
- prepare FAQs and known issue guidance
- brief frontline support staff before launch
- define who owns issue triage during the first weeks

## Learning hub guidance

A central learning hub should include:

- what is changing and why
- training recordings or schedules
- quick-start guides
- FAQs
- support contacts
- release updates and known issues

## Common mistakes

- training too early, then expecting people to remember everything at launch
- only training end users and forgetting managers, admins, or support staff
- measuring attendance but not capability
- offering support channels without clear ownership

## Review questions

- Do different user groups have different training paths?
- Can users perform the critical day-one tasks after training?
- Is support staffed and visible before launch?
- Does the learning hub act as a reliable source of truth?
