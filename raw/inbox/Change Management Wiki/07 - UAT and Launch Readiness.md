# UAT and Launch Readiness

## Purpose

Use this page to validate the minimum viable experience before launch and make a disciplined go/no-go decision based on business-critical workflows.

## Best practices

- Start by identifying real pain points across departments, not just technical requirements.
- Prioritize departments and use cases based on business impact and readiness.
- Define an MVP that covers the majority of daily work and critical workflows.
- Run UAT with champions, admins, power users, and representatives from each key department.
- Set explicit go/no-go criteria before testing begins.

## Good UAT inputs

- department pain points
- slow, broken, or error-prone workflows
- priority use cases by team
- integrations and permissions that must work
- business rules or compliance requirements

## MVP guidance

An MVP for launch should:

- support the most common daily tasks
- cover critical workflows end to end
- avoid non-essential complexity
- include enough support content for users to succeed

## Who to include in UAT

- at least one representative from each high-impact department
- business SMEs who know the workflow details
- technical SMEs who understand dependencies
- champions or power users who can give practical feedback
- admins or support owners who will handle real issues after launch

## What to test

- top use cases by department
- integrations and handoffs
- permissions and access
- notifications and approvals
- reporting, audit, or compliance requirements
- support process for common failure scenarios

## Go/no-go criteria examples

- all critical workflows complete successfully
- no unresolved severity-one or severity-two defects
- support team is trained and staffed
- training and communications are ready
- data, permissions, and integrations are validated

## Common mistakes

- calling it UAT when only the project team tests
- validating features but not end-to-end workflows
- launching with unclear defect thresholds
- allowing the MVP to expand until testing becomes unstable

## Review questions

- Does the MVP cover the workflows that matter most on day one?
- Are the right departments represented in UAT?
- Have we defined launch blockers in advance?
- Can we explain why we are ready, not just hope we are ready?
