# Adoption Monitoring

## Purpose

Use this page after launch to monitor usage, understand adoption barriers, and drive continuous improvement with evidence instead of assumptions.

## Best practices

- Monitor both usage signals and outcome signals.
- Combine quantitative data with feedback from surveys, managers, and support channels.
- Identify low-adoption groups quickly and respond with targeted action.
- Share progress regularly with sponsors and department leaders.
- Treat adoption shortfalls as a design and support problem first, not a blame problem.

## What to monitor

### Usage indicators

- active users in target groups
- completion of critical workflows
- drop-off points in the new process
- use of legacy tools or workarounds

### Experience indicators

- support ticket volume and themes
- training follow-up questions
- survey responses
- manager observations

### Business indicators

- speed, quality, or compliance improvements
- fewer errors or rework loops
- better visibility of work or decisions

## What to do if adoption is low

- run targeted communications for affected groups
- offer refresher training or office hours
- simplify guidance for the workflows users struggle with
- increase manager and sponsor reinforcement
- investigate whether process design, tooling, or policy is causing friction

## Reporting guidance

Report on:

- adoption by audience or department
- wins and friction points
- actions underway
- decisions needed from leaders

Use a regular cadence such as weekly during early rollout, then monthly once the change stabilizes.

## 30-60-90 day review pattern

- 30 days: detect friction, support gaps, and misunderstanding
- 60 days: compare adoption by group and reinforce lagging areas
- 90 days: review whether the change is delivering the intended business value

## Common mistakes

- measuring login counts and calling that success
- waiting too long to react to low adoption
- failing to close the loop on user feedback
- stopping executive attention immediately after go-live

## Review questions

- Are we measuring behavior change, not just access?
- Do we know which groups are struggling and why?
- Are leaders seeing the right metrics at the right cadence?
- Have we turned feedback into visible improvements?
