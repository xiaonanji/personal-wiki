# Change Management Wiki

This wiki converts the ideas in `Change management template.pdf` into reusable best-practice notes for planning and delivering organizational change.

## How to use this wiki

- Start with the foundations page to frame the change correctly.
- Use the vision and governance pages before you design rollout activity.
- Use the communications, training, and UAT pages to prepare launch.
- Use the adoption page after go-live to keep momentum and improve outcomes.

## Core principles

- Define the business reason for change before discussing tooling.
- Make the end state concrete enough that teams can picture success.
- Assign visible ownership, sponsorship, and decision rights early.
- Choose a rollout style that matches urgency, risk, and user readiness.
- Repeat communication across multiple channels and manager layers.
- Design training around what people need to know, do, and feel.
- Validate critical workflows with real users before launch.
- Treat adoption as an ongoing practice, not a one-time event.

## Lightweight end-to-end checklist

- Clarify why the change is happening, what is changing, and who is affected.
- Define the vision, benefits, success measures, and risks of failure.
- Build a rollout team with a sponsor, business owner, technical owner, and SMEs.
- Choose rollout style, priority teams, and the first use cases to support.
- Prepare audience-specific communications with clear cadence and owners.
- Create role-based training, support channels, and a learning hub.
- Run UAT on the MVP with representatives from critical departments.
- Set go/no-go criteria, launch, monitor adoption, and adjust quickly.

## Notes on source material

The source PDF is template-driven and Atlassian-oriented in places, but the practices in this wiki are written to be tool-agnostic so they can be reused for technology, process, or operating-model changes.
