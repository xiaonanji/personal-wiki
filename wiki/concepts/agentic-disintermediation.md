# Agentic Disintermediation

## Summary

- The source argues that many businesses earn rents from human friction rather than from irreplaceable value.
- AI agents reduce search, comparison, negotiation, and execution costs, which compresses those rents.

## Definition

- Agentic disintermediation: the removal or repricing of intermediaries when AI agents can perform discovery, comparison, negotiation, routing, and task execution on behalf of users or firms.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[sources/2028-global-intelligence-crisis]] highlights pressure on SaaS renewals, travel booking, insurance renewals, card-payment fees, customer service software, and real-estate commissions.
- The source's diagrams and examples consistently frame friction as the moat that agents erode.
- The claimed effect is not only substitution of labor, but destruction of business models built on inertia and information asymmetry.

## Counterpoints

- Some intermediaries may survive by bundling trust, compliance, distribution, or capital rather than mere convenience.
- The source is stronger on mechanism than on boundary conditions for where the effect stops.
