# Ghost GDP

## Summary

- Ghost GDP is the source's label for output that appears in GDP and profit statistics but does not circulate through households strongly enough to sustain the human economy built around wage income and consumption.

## Definition

- Ghost GDP: measured economic output that rises because AI systems produce more at lower labor cost, while the associated income fails to flow broadly enough into household spending and tax receipts.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[sources/2028-global-intelligence-crisis]] argues nominal GDP and productivity can remain strong while wages stagnate and white-collar layoffs rise.
- The source ties the concept to slower money velocity and weaker discretionary consumption.
- It also links the idea to fiscal stress: output exists, but less of it passes through taxable labor income.

## Counterpoints

- The term is a framing device from the source, not a standard macroeconomic category.
- The argument depends on distributional bottlenecks being stronger than offsetting effects such as lower prices, new spending categories, or capital recycling.
