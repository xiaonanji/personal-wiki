# Intelligence Displacement Spiral

## Summary

- This is the source's central feedback-loop model.
- AI displaces white-collar labor, displaced workers reduce spending or accept lower wages, weaker demand hurts businesses and credit quality, and firms respond by buying even more AI.

## Definition

- Intelligence displacement spiral: a negative feedback loop in which AI-driven labor displacement weakens incomes, consumption, and collateral quality, which then increases pressure for further automation and deepens the downturn.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[sources/2028-global-intelligence-crisis]] links white-collar layoffs to wage compression in services and gig work.
- The source extends the loop into private credit, life insurers, mortgage stress, municipal finance, and fiscal-policy strain.
- Its mortgage argument is especially important: prime borrowers become risky because future income persistence breaks down.

## Counterpoints

- The loop is a scenario claim and may overstate how synchronized these channels would become.
- The speed of policy response, job creation in complementary sectors, and labor-market adaptation remains unresolved.
