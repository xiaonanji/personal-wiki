# Point-by-Point Reset

## Summary

- The source argues that long-run stable performance depends on resetting emotionally after each attempt instead of carrying the previous result into the next one.

## Definition

- Point-by-point reset: a deliberate mental transition in which the previous action is treated as finished and attention is re-centered on the next action.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[sources/stable-performance-is-key-to-winning]] describes a Federer-style pattern: brief emotional release, then a clear return to focus.
- The source treats emotional control as a core mechanism for preserving a slight long-run edge.
- It extends the same logic from tennis points to life decisions, work tasks, and error recovery.

## Counterpoints

- The concept is useful as a performance heuristic, but it does not by itself solve strategic or skill deficits.
- Some forms of reflection require more than a reset; they require deliberate review after the event.
