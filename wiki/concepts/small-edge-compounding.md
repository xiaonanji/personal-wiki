# Small-Edge Compounding

## Summary

- This concept is the idea that a slight but repeatable advantage at the smallest unit of action can compound into large differences in long-run outcomes.

## Definitions

- Small edge: being only slightly better than the baseline or opponent in each local attempt.
- Compounding: repeated slight advantages accumulating into a much larger aggregate result over time.

## Supporting Evidence

- [[sources/stable-performance-is-key-to-winning]] uses Roger Federer's reported 54% point-win rate versus 82% match-win rate as the core illustration.
- The source extends the same logic to work and life: slightly better handling of many small tasks or decisions can separate top performers from average performers.

## Counterpoints

- Some fields are dominated by structural asymmetries, leverage, or winner-take-all dynamics where a small local edge is not sufficient.
- The concept can become misleading if used to ignore the importance of strategy, environment, and resource constraints.
