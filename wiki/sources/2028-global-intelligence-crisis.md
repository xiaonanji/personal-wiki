# The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis

## Source Details

- Type: translated web clip of a macro/financial thought exercise
- Authors named in clip: Citrini Research and [[entities/alap-shah]]
- Original title: *THE 2028 GLOBAL INTELLIGENCE CRISIS: A Thought Exercise in Financial History, from the Future*
- Clip language: Chinese translation with introductory framing
- Raw path: `raw/inbox/2026-04-06T121135+1000 转载《2028年全球智能危机----来自未来的金融史思想实验》.md`

## Summary

- The piece is explicitly framed as a thought experiment, not a prediction.
- It imagines a 2026-2028 crisis in which rapid AI capability growth boosts measured productivity while hollowing out white-collar labor income and downstream consumer demand.
- The core mechanism is a compounding feedback loop: stronger AI reduces labor demand, weaker labor income hurts consumption and credit quality, weaker demand pushes firms to automate further, and the loop spills from software into payments, private credit, housing, and fiscal policy.
- The source treats many business models as fragile because they depend on human friction, inertia, information asymmetry, or coordination costs that AI agents can remove.

## Key Claims

- [[concepts/ai-efficiency-paradox]]: AI success can coincide with economic instability rather than broad prosperity.
- [[concepts/ghost-gdp]]: output can rise on paper while household purchasing power and money circulation weaken.
- [[concepts/agentic-disintermediation]]: AI agents compress margins in SaaS, travel, insurance, payments, and other intermediated categories by removing friction.
- [[concepts/intelligence-displacement-spiral]]: labor displacement, consumption weakness, credit stress, and further automation reinforce one another.
- Prime mortgage books become risky not because borrowers were weak at origination, but because their income assumptions become structurally unreliable after origination.
- Policy institutions lag because tax, welfare, and stabilization systems assume temporary unemployment rather than structural labor displacement.

## Structure And Evidence Inside The Source

- `序言`: sets up the document as a scenario exercise rather than a forecast.
- `备忘录 智力过剩的后果`: introduces the macro loop, unemployment shock, ghost GDP, and credit implications.
- `如何开始`: argues coding agents and internal build-vs-buy pressure start the cascade in software.
- `当摩擦力变为零时`: argues consumer agents erode rents in travel, insurance, payments, legal, tax, and real estate.
- `从行业风险到系统性风险`: argues white-collar displacement is macro-significant because white-collar workers anchor discretionary consumption.
- `智力置换螺旋`: describes wage compression from displaced professionals moving into lower-wage service work.
- `相关赌注的链式关系`: links SaaS repricing to private-credit stress and insurer exposure.
- `抵押贷款问题`: reframes prime mortgage risk around future income uncertainty rather than borrower quality at origination.
- `与时间的战斗`: argues fiscal institutions lag the speed of AI-driven structural change.

## Source Reliability Notes

- The clip repeatedly says the document is a thought experiment.
- It mixes real companies and institutions with future-dated scenario datapoints, so individual statistics inside the scenario should not be treated as verified reporting.
- This wiki should treat the source primarily as a model of risk mechanisms, not as direct evidence that the described 2028 crisis will occur.
