# Change Management Wiki Bundle

## Source Details

- Type: bundled best-practice notes
- Source system: local markdown wiki bundle in `raw/inbox/Change Management Wiki/`
- Original PDF source: `raw/inbox/Change Management Wiki/Change management template.pdf`
- Derived bundle: the markdown files in the same folder appear to decompose or rewrite the original PDF into topical notes
- Topic: practical organizational change planning, rollout, launch readiness, and post-launch adoption

## Summary

- This source bundle turns a template-driven change-management PDF into short, tool-agnostic operating notes for organizational change delivery.
- The bundle treats change management as an end-to-end workstream rather than a communications-only task.
- Its structure follows the lifecycle from initial framing through vision, sponsorship, rollout design, communications, training, UAT, launch readiness, and ongoing adoption monitoring.
- The guidance is practical and checklist-oriented rather than framework-heavy; it favors reusable planning prompts, role definitions, and review questions over formal theory.

## Key Claims

- [[concepts/organizational-change-management]]: change management should begin with problem framing, business rationale, impact scope, and accountable ownership before teams argue about tooling or features.
- Success should be defined as behavior and outcome change, not merely technical go-live.
- [[concepts/change-sponsorship-and-governance]]: visible sponsorship, clear decision rights, manager reinforcement, and early use of SMEs and champions materially affect rollout quality.
- Rollout scope should be defined around critical use cases and audience readiness rather than launching every feature to every team at once.
- Training and support should be role-based and designed around what people need to know, do, and feel to succeed.
- [[concepts/change-adoption-monitoring]]: adoption is a continuing measurement and intervention loop, not a one-time launch milestone.

## Notable Evidence

### Bundle structure

- `01 - Foundations.md`: framing the change, scope of impact, and accountable owner
- `02 - Vision and Success.md`: user-centered end state, benefits, metrics, and mindset shifts
- `03 - Governance and Sponsorship.md`: sponsor, change lead, business owner, technical owner, champions, and manager roles
- `04 - Rollout Strategy.md`: big-bang versus phased rollout tradeoffs and initial-scope logic
- `05 - Communications Plan.md`: audience-specific messaging, channels, timing, and resistance handling
- `06 - Training and Support.md`: role-based training design, learning outcomes, and support channels
- `07 - UAT and Launch Readiness.md`: MVP focus, critical-workflow validation, and go/no-go criteria
- `08 - Adoption Monitoring.md`: usage, experience, and business signals plus a 30-60-90 day review cadence

### Practical artifacts recommended across the bundle

- one-paragraph change summary
- impacted audience list
- named change lead plus sponsor/owner roles
- risk, assumptions, issues, and dependencies log
- audience-specific communications plan
- learning hub with guides, FAQs, recordings, support contacts, and release updates
- explicit go/no-go criteria tied to critical workflows

## Contradictions Or Tensions

- The bundle consistently argues against treating go-live as success, which creates a useful tension with delivery cultures that stop executive attention once the technology is launched.
- The bundle is tool-agnostic and concise, which increases reuse but also means it does not fully address specialized regulatory, unionized, or highly safety-critical contexts.
